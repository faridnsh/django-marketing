# Django Audience Jobs-to-be-Done Research Brief

## Scope

Research the assigned audience as a potential evaluator, adopter, user, recommender, teacher, supporter, contributor, or funder of Django. This phase is exclusively about audiences and their jobs.

Do not inventory Django marketing, assess existing Django pages or campaigns, recommend assets, perform detailed competitor analysis, create positioning or messaging, propose campaigns, or recommend a djangoproject.com redesign.

The supplied label and existing analysis are hypotheses. Rename, split, merge, or classify the audience as a context when evidence warrants it. People can occupy multiple audiences. For organizations, identify the initiator, researcher/evaluator, influencer, approver/economic buyer, blockers/reviewers, users, and people bearing consequences.

Distinguish the audience's underlying job from a Django-desired action. Jobs cannot be “use Django,” “learn Django,” or “visit the website.”

## Evidence standard

- Browse and cite diverse public evidence. Prefer recent sources, but use older sources for persistent patterns.
- Avoid relying primarily on Django-controlled sources. Django/DSF sources are acceptable for governance, process, and factual project details, but triangulate audience motivations externally.
- Prefer primary/authoritative evidence: survey reports and datasets, peer-reviewed or industry research, interview studies, direct adoption/migration/postmortem accounts, curricula and procurement guidance, maintainer studies, job-market datasets, conference talks, and substantive community discussions.
- Treat an isolated forum or social post as a qualitative signal, never representative proof.
- Link to the exact source and give publication date/year when available. Do not cite search-result pages.
- For each major conclusion, state whether it is direct evidence or inference and assign High, Medium, or Low confidence. Explain gaps.
- Do not invent precision or rank criteria unless evidence supports ranking.
- Separate practical concerns, perceived risks, misconceptions, legitimate limitations, organizational objections, and emotional resistance.
- Explain the behavioral role of channels: discovery, evaluation, validation, or ongoing engagement. Do not merely list popular channels.

## Required report structure

Use every heading below:

1. `# Audience label`

6. `## Functional, emotional, and social dimensions` (label hypotheses)
7. `## Triggering situations` (distinguish recurring and event-triggered)
8. `## Desired outcomes` (directionally measurable where possible)
9. `## Current behaviour or status quo`
10. `## Pushes`
11. `## Pulls`
12. `## Anxieties`
13. `## Habits and inertia`
14. `## Decision criteria` (importance and confidence; rank only with evidence)
15. `## Main concerns`
16. `## Objections and perceived risks`
17. `## Information needed to make progress`
18. `## Trusted content formats`
19. `## Discovery, evaluation, validation, and engagement channels`
20. `## Decision-makers and influencers`

26. `## Unanswered research questions`

For job hierarchy, associate each important job with core functional, emotional, social, and supporting jobs. Only include emotional/social jobs when evidenced or explicitly marked as hypotheses.

The evidence table columns must be: Finding | Source (title, publisher/author, date, URL) | Evidence type | Direct evidence or inference | Confidence | Research notes.

## Existing-analysis hypotheses to audit

Audit every relevant claim below as Supported, Partially supported, Unsupported, Contradicted, or Requires further research. Ignore irrelevant claims. Preserve descriptive segment labels, not fictional persona names.

- Donors include individuals/small donors and organizations; individuals want tangible outcomes such as Fellowship maintenance and community grants; corporate funders need visible impact and internal ROI/proposal support.
- A mid-to-large-company “marketing executive or technical decision-maker” approves framework standards based on currency/relevance, long-term reliance, community/support, security, and hiring; enterprise examples, production architecture, and testimonials influence them.
- Early-career/self-taught/bootcamp/CS/career-changing developers evaluate a framework as a career investment, portfolio tool, modern-app tool, and route to a welcoming community; homepage/social/video/community discovery is claimed.
- Existing professional Django developers seek current best practice, releases/upgrades, scaling help, greater expertise, and possible progression from user to contributor.
- Ecosystem/core contributors seek recognition, governance understanding and influence, co-maintainers, less burnout, and a clear participation pathway.
- Companies depending on Django want maintenance continuity to avoid migration costs and may fund it; corporate approval can involve finance and leadership.
- Claimed touchpoints/channels include docs/release notes, forums/Discord, GitHub/issue tracker, mailing lists, conferences/sprints, podcasts, professional peer networks, and short-form social. Validate behavioral use; do not assume.

## Writing constraints

Keep the report audience-centric. Generic alternatives may be mentioned only to explain status quo or switching forces. Do not rank competitors. Avoid claims about missing marketing assets. Acknowledge evidence limitations. Aim for a substantive but focused report (roughly 2,500–4,500 words plus evidence table).
