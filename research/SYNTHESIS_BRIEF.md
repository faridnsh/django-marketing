# Cross-audience synthesis assignment

Read `RESEARCH_BRIEF.md` and all 17 completed files in `audiences/` before drawing conclusions. Do not merely concatenate or summarize the reports. Reconcile contradictions, respect confidence labels, and distinguish evidence-backed findings from synthesis inference.

This remains an audience/JTBD phase. Do not create a marketing inventory, competitor analysis, positioning, messaging, campaign/channel strategy, asset-gap analysis, or website recommendations.

Create these files:

1. `synthesis/01-cross-audience-synthesis.md`
   - Audience architecture across experience, adoption role, organization/context, industry overlay, Django lifecycle, ecosystem participation, and funding relationship.
   - Shared job clusters, with participating audiences, meaningful variations, evidence strength, and important exceptions.
   - Audience-specific jobs that genuinely distinguish a lens.
   - Overlaps and duplication, including candidates to merge, split, rename, or treat as context.
   - Journey/lifecycle transition hypotheses, clearly separating evidenced transitions from hypotheses.
   - A decision-system map showing how practitioners, evaluators, leaders, risk/procurement stakeholders, educators, contributors/maintainers, and funders influence one another.
   - Evidence gaps and a concise synthesis of the primary-research implications.

2. `synthesis/02-audience-job-taxonomy.md`
   - A usable multi-dimensional taxonomy, not one mutually exclusive persona list.
   - Define each dimension and categories; show how to compose an audience/job context.
   - Map all 17 supplied lenses into the taxonomy.
   - Include a normalized job taxonomy: job cluster, job statement/progress, contexts, participants/roles, desired outcomes, and confidence.

3. `synthesis/03-segmentation-changes.md`
   - One prioritized list of labels to retain, rename, split, merge, or convert to contextual overlays.
   - Explain evidence, implications for future research (not marketing execution), confidence, and what must be validated.

4. `synthesis/04-evidence-gaps.md`
   - Assess strength and bias by audience and job cluster.
   - Identify missing rejecter/non-adopter/former-user evidence, survivorship/self-selection/geographic/language biases, role attribution problems, channel-behavior gaps, and weak causal evidence.
   - Distinguish gaps resolvable by desk research, surveys/data analysis, interviews, contextual inquiry, and decision-episode reconstruction.

5. `synthesis/05-primary-research-plan.md`
   - Prioritized interview/research groups and concrete research questions.
   - Include decision-episode sampling, recruitment characteristics, comparison cases, sample-size ranges as research design guidance (not statistical precision), sequencing, and what decision each study will inform.
   - Prioritize uncertainty × likely consequence; do not propose campaigns or content/assets.

6. `synthesis/06-carry-forward-hypotheses.md`
   - A clear, bounded set of hypotheses for later inventory, alternatives, positioning, and audience-prioritization phases.
   - Each hypothesis needs provenance (audience reports/findings), current confidence, key caveat, and the next phase or primary research that can test it.
   - Do not turn hypotheses into final positioning or messaging.

Use citations to the underlying audience reports and, for important external factual claims, retain direct source links where practical. Explicitly state when a cross-audience pattern is synthesis inference.
