# Independent critical-review assignment

Read `RESEARCH_BRIEF.md`, every audience report, every synthesis deliverable, and this brief. Challenge the combined research rather than restating it.

Review for:

- unsupported generalizations and invented motivations;
- overreliance on stereotypes or title-based assumptions;
- confusion between jobs and solutions/actions;
- confusion between audiences, roles, lifecycle stages, organization types, and industry contexts;
- duplicate or falsely unified segments;
- channel claims without behavioral evidence;
- Django-centric rather than audience-centric framing;
- conclusions based on isolated anecdotes or incumbent/self-selected samples;
- premature competitor, positioning, inventory, asset, messaging, campaign, channel-strategy, or website analysis;
- desired Django actions that do not follow from the audience's job;
- inconsistent confidence ratings, missing counterevidence, circular reliance on Django sources, and citation-quality problems;
- failures to audit relevant supplied-analysis claims.

Lead structural QA found that several audience reports name 3–7 additional jobs in tables or numbered lists but do not restate every job in the requested `When …, I want to …, so I can …` form. Audit all 17 reports for this, not just the most obvious cases (02, 10, 13, 17), and add concise audience-progress statements where needed without changing the evidence or turning actions into jobs.

Write `review/critical-review.md` with:

1. Executive verdict and fitness-for-purpose assessment.
2. Scope-compliance audit.
3. Cross-cutting methodological issues.
4. Findings requiring correction now (file/section, problem, evidence/reason, exact correction).
5. Findings that should remain explicit hypotheses pending primary research.
6. Audience-by-audience review matrix.
7. Synthesis/taxonomy/segmentation/evidence-gap/research-plan review.
8. Desired-action logic audit.
9. Citation and evidence-quality audit.
10. Residual risks and final acceptance checklist.

Apply straightforward, evidence-preserving corrections directly to the affected research files when warranted. Do not silently upgrade confidence or invent evidence. Record every material edit in a final “Corrections applied” section. If a problem cannot be corrected from existing evidence, mark it for primary research rather than guessing.
