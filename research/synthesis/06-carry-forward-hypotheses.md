# Carry-forward hypotheses

## Use and limits

These are bounded propositions for later inventory, alternatives, positioning, and audience-prioritization phases or for primary research. They are not findings about market size, final positioning, messages, channels, or recommended assets. A later phase may reject them. Confidence reflects current evidence; provenance links to the audience reports where the underlying findings and external sources are documented.

## Hypotheses for a later inventory phase

### H1 — Readiness transitions may matter more than a generic beginner/professional divide

**Hypothesis:** The consequential transitions are no-code → small-program independence, Python-capable → web-system model, and web-capable → safe Django delivery; treating all three as “beginner” will obscure different jobs and failure points.

**Provenance:** Readiness boundary and scaffolded first-project evidence in [first-time programmers](../audiences/01-first-time-programmers.md); Python/web boundary and deployment cliff in [Python developers new to web](../audiences/02-python-developers-new-to-web.md). **Current confidence:** High structural, Medium on exact thresholds. **Key caveat:** the reports do not measure who reaches each transition or which Django encounter caused it. **Test:** Study 3 observation and longitudinal transfer tasks; a later inventory can then code which readiness/job transition each existing item actually serves without judging quality here.

### H2 — People need different evidence at discovery, evaluation, validation, approval, and ongoing engagement

**Hypothesis:** A source or interaction useful for first awareness is not automatically trusted for workload validation, organizational approval, operation, contribution, or funding renewal.

**Provenance:** Behavioral channel distinctions recur across [backend engineers](../audiences/03-experienced-backend-engineers.md), [technical leads](../audiences/05-technical-leads-software-architects.md), [large organizations](../audiences/08-large-organizations-stakeholders.md), and [contributors](../audiences/15-prospective-existing-django-contributors.md). **Current confidence:** Medium-High as a stage distinction; Low on actual channel effects. **Key caveat:** current evidence mostly reports channel use, not behavior change. **Test:** the cross-study channel timeline module; later inventory should wait for episode evidence before assigning behavioral roles.

### H3 — Lifecycle stewardship is a distinct information job from initial evaluation

**Hypothesis:** Operating, upgrading, securing, and evolving an incumbent system requires evidence and decision support that cannot be inferred from greenfield adoption jobs.

**Provenance:** Safe-change and dependency-stack jobs in [existing users](../audiences/13-existing-django-users.md); lifecycle stewardship in [technical leads](../audiences/05-technical-leads-software-architects.md) and [large organizations](../audiences/08-large-organizations-stakeholders.md). **Current confidence:** High. **Key caveat:** typical operating burden and silent legacy estates are not measured. **Test:** Studies 1 and 2; later inventory can distinguish greenfield proof, onboarding, operation, upgrade, and reconsideration evidence.

### H4 — Teaching support must be assessed at both instructor and institution levels

**Hypothesis:** The materials/processes needed to teach, observe, intervene, assess, and keep a course runnable differ from the evidence needed to approve and sustain a curriculum portfolio.

**Provenance:** Individual teaching jobs in [educators](../audiences/12-individual-educators-instructors-bootcamp-teachers.md); standards, capacity, demand, and economics in [institutional curriculum decision-makers](../audiences/17-educational-institutions-training-curriculum-decision-makers.md). **Current confidence:** High functional. **Key caveat:** framework selector and teacher authority vary by institution. **Test:** Study 8 paired course episodes; later inventory can code the decision role and course lifecycle served.

## Hypotheses for a later alternatives analysis

### H5 — “Alternative” includes no-build, buy, incumbent continuation, internal platform, and partial architecture changes

**Hypothesis:** Real alternatives are not limited to other web frameworks; teams may buy a service, postpone, keep the incumbent, use an organizational platform, isolate a workload, add a separate client, decompose, or archive.

**Provenance:** Contextual choice logic in [backend engineers](../audiences/03-experienced-backend-engineers.md), [startups](../audiences/07-startups-technical-decision-makers.md), [large organizations](../audiences/08-large-organizations-stakeholders.md), [former users](../audiences/14-former-django-users.md), and [maintainers](../audiences/11-django-python-package-maintainers.md). **Current confidence:** High. **Key caveat:** public evidence overrepresents technology-to-technology migrations. **Test:** Studies 1 and 2 with explicit no-build/buy/continue recruitment; later alternatives analysis should compare progress paths, not only named frameworks.

### H6 — Integrated capabilities trade assembly cost now against coupled change later

**Hypothesis:** Django’s integrated conventions may reduce search, integration, and coordination for common data-centric work while increasing vocabulary, hidden behavior, or coupled upgrade/change costs in some contexts.

**Provenance:** Double-edged beginner abstraction in [01](../audiences/01-first-time-programmers.md); first-stack integration and deployment in [02](../audiences/02-python-developers-new-to-web.md); cross-layer seams in [full-stack developers](../audiences/04-full-stack-developers.md); upgrade/changeability in [existing users](../audiences/13-existing-django-users.md). **Current confidence:** Medium-High mechanism, Low on net effect by context. **Key caveat:** integration costs are confounded with architecture, packages, team topology, and experience. **Test:** Studies 3 and 4 plus matched delivery episodes; a later alternatives phase can compare total workflow and lifecycle consequences rather than feature counts.

### H7 — Workload and interaction model, not generic “scale,” determine many technical fit decisions

**Hypothesis:** Conventional data-centric workflows, API platforms, rich client interaction, realtime/async I/O, latency sensitivity, and legacy integration create different showstoppers and mitigations; traffic volume alone is an inadequate segment.

**Provenance:** Workload segmentation in [backend engineers](../audiences/03-experienced-backend-engineers.md), [full-stack developers](../audiences/04-full-stack-developers.md), [technical leads](../audiences/05-technical-leads-software-architects.md), and [existing users](../audiences/13-existing-django-users.md). **Current confidence:** High structural; Low on frequency of decisive mismatches. **Key caveat:** public large-scale accounts are atypical. **Test:** Studies 1 and 4 using representative critical paths; later alternatives analysis should avoid one universal performance hierarchy.

### H8 — Familiarity is both a legitimate productivity resource and a source of inertia

**Hypothesis:** Existing knowledge, tooling, and team practices can make Django the lowest-risk choice for one episode while also discouraging a warranted reassessment; the direction depends on current fit and switching cost.

**Provenance:** Habits and installed practices across [full-stack developers](../audiences/04-full-stack-developers.md), [startups](../audiences/07-startups-technical-decision-makers.md), [existing users](../audiences/13-existing-django-users.md), and return/departure accounts in [former users](../audiences/14-former-django-users.md). **Current confidence:** Medium-High. **Key caveat:** retrospective return stories especially overstate familiarity-driven speed. **Test:** Study 2 matched returner/non-returner and stayer/leaver episodes; later alternatives work should include transition cost and learning rather than treating familiarity as bias alone.

### H9 — Framework fit and ecosystem-package risk are related but separable decisions

**Hypothesis:** For many production choices, aggregate package compatibility, maintenance, security, and ownership may be as consequential as Django core characteristics.

**Provenance:** Package/lifecycle concerns in [technical leads](../audiences/05-technical-leads-software-architects.md), [large organizations](../audiences/08-large-organizations-stakeholders.md), [package maintainers](../audiences/11-django-python-package-maintainers.md), and [existing users](../audiences/13-existing-django-users.md). **Current confidence:** High that the job exists, Low on relative contribution to rejection or migration. **Key caveat:** package criticality and maintainer capacity are poorly measured. **Test:** Studies 1 and 6 with dependency inventories and decision artifacts; later alternatives analysis should record core and package-stack evidence separately.

## Hypotheses for a later positioning phase

### H10 — A single “Django is fast” claim would collapse distinct meanings of progress

**Hypothesis:** Relevant “speed” can mean time to first mastery, time to a production-capable slice, fewer cross-layer handoffs, onboarding time, change lead time, incident recovery, or upgrade effort; audiences will not treat these as interchangeable.

**Provenance:** Mastery in [01](../audiences/01-first-time-programmers.md), deployable first app in [02](../audiences/02-python-developers-new-to-web.md), end-to-end delivery in [04](../audiences/04-full-stack-developers.md), validated learning in [07](../audiences/07-startups-technical-decision-makers.md), and safe change in [13](../audiences/13-existing-django-users.md). **Current confidence:** High conceptual. **Key caveat:** no cross-audience study establishes which metric is decisive or Django’s comparative effect. **Test:** Studies 1, 3, 4, and 5; later positioning must use measured progress contexts rather than a universal speed assertion.

### H11 — “Batteries included” is likely a context-dependent mechanism, not a self-explanatory benefit

**Hypothesis:** Integrated capabilities are valued when they remove undifferentiated assembly and clarify a common path, but can create cognitive load for novices or feel constraining where the interaction/workload requires different boundaries.

**Provenance:** [First-time programmers](../audiences/01-first-time-programmers.md), [Python developers new to web](../audiences/02-python-developers-new-to-web.md), [full-stack developers](../audiences/04-full-stack-developers.md), [startups](../audiences/07-startups-technical-decision-makers.md), and [former users](../audiences/14-former-django-users.md). **Current confidence:** Medium-High. **Key caveat:** current-user testimony and migration anecdotes do not establish comparative preference. **Test:** Studies 3 and 4 plus later alternatives work; positioning research should test interpretation and boundary conditions, not assume the phrase communicates progress.

### H12 — Maturity creates both assurance and perceived stagnation

**Hypothesis:** Long-lived governance, release/security processes, ecosystem experience, and installed use may reduce lifecycle uncertainty for some evaluators, while some practitioners interpret the same maturity as stale or less career-relevant.

**Provenance:** Lifecycle assurance in [technical leads](../audiences/05-technical-leads-software-architects.md) and [large organizations](../audiences/08-large-organizations-stakeholders.md); perceived modernity/career anxieties in [full-stack developers](../audiences/04-full-stack-developers.md) and [Python-to-web entrants](../audiences/02-python-developers-new-to-web.md). **Current confidence:** Medium for the tension, Low on prevalence and behavioral consequence. **Key caveat:** social/status jobs are often inferred and local labor markets vary. **Test:** Studies 1, 3, and 4, plus region-specific labor data in a later phase; do not convert this into a maturity/modernity message without evidence.

### H13 — Trust claims require role-specific proof and explicit consequence boundaries

**Hypothesis:** Practitioners, architects, service owners, security reviewers, curriculum committees, clients, and funders use different evidence to grant trust; popularity or a production example alone will rarely satisfy every role.

**Provenance:** Defensible proof in [technical leads](../audiences/05-technical-leads-software-architects.md); cross-functional approval in [large organizations](../audiences/08-large-organizations-stakeholders.md); multi-party client trust in [agencies](../audiences/10-agencies-consultancies.md); program credibility in [institutions](../audiences/17-educational-institutions-training-curriculum-decision-makers.md); accountability in [funders](../audiences/16-donors-sponsors-corporate-members.md). **Current confidence:** High functional, Low on exact proof hierarchy. **Key caveat:** guidance sources describe what should matter more often than what changed real choices. **Test:** Studies 1, 5, 7, and 8; later positioning research must specify role, decision, and evidence standard.

### H14 — Community can support different jobs without being one universal adoption driver

**Hypothesis:** Respectful help may support learner persistence, peer evidence may reduce evaluator uncertainty, contribution may create belonging/influence, and funding may express reciprocity; these mechanisms should not be collapsed into “community matters.”

**Provenance:** Belonging hypotheses in [01](../audiences/01-first-time-programmers.md); help channels in [02](../audiences/02-python-developers-new-to-web.md); contributor legitimacy in [15](../audiences/15-prospective-existing-django-contributors.md); reciprocity and accountability in [16](../audiences/16-donors-sponsors-corporate-members.md). **Current confidence:** Medium for distinct mechanisms, Low on causal strength. **Key caveat:** engaged-community samples and program selection bias are severe. **Test:** Studies 3, 6, and 7 with unsuccessful/non-participating comparisons; later positioning must not imply one community promise fits all.

## Hypotheses for a later audience-prioritization phase

### H15 — Decision role and lifecycle may predict research needs better than title or organization size

**Hypothesis:** Evaluators in different-sized organizations may share more jobs than an evaluator and approver with the same title; incumbent/reconsidering state may matter more than “enterprise” or “developer.”

**Provenance:** Segmentation problems across [backend engineers](../audiences/03-experienced-backend-engineers.md), [technical leads](../audiences/05-technical-leads-software-architects.md), [engineering leaders](../audiences/06-ctos-engineering-managers.md), [large organizations](../audiences/08-large-organizations-stakeholders.md), and [existing users](../audiences/13-existing-django-users.md). **Current confidence:** High conceptual. **Key caveat:** better explanatory taxonomy does not establish size, consequence, or reachability. **Test:** Study 1 cross-case coding and a later survey/data phase; audience prioritization should compare composed contexts rather than source labels.

### H16 — Educators, maintainers, contributors, and funders influence downstream capacity but should not be valued only as acquisition intermediaries

**Hypothesis:** These groups have independent success criteria—learning outcomes, honest maintenance scope, legitimate bounded participation, and accountable stewardship—while also affecting practitioner capability and ecosystem continuity.

**Provenance:** [Educators](../audiences/12-individual-educators-instructors-bootcamp-teachers.md), [maintainers](../audiences/11-django-python-package-maintainers.md), [contributors](../audiences/15-prospective-existing-django-contributors.md), [funders](../audiences/16-donors-sponsors-corporate-members.md), and [institutions](../audiences/17-educational-institutions-training-curriculum-decision-makers.md). **Current confidence:** High structural. **Key caveat:** downstream influence and causal leverage are not quantified. **Test:** Studies 6–8 and later ecosystem/network analysis; prioritization must score direct job consequence separately from hypothesized downstream influence.

### H17 — Rejecters, quiet leavers, and no-build cases may change the apparent ranking of needs

**Hypothesis:** Findings drawn mainly from current users overstate jobs compatible with Django and understate elimination criteria, opportunity cost, inaccessible pathways, and rational reasons not to adopt or continue.

**Provenance:** Explicit survivorship cautions in [02](../audiences/02-python-developers-new-to-web.md), [05](../audiences/05-technical-leads-software-architects.md), [06](../audiences/06-ctos-engineering-managers.md), [13](../audiences/13-existing-django-users.md), and [14](../audiences/14-former-django-users.md). **Current confidence:** High that bias exists, unknown effect size/direction by job. **Key caveat:** rejecters are heterogeneous and rejection may mean successful fit assessment. **Test:** Studies 1 and 2 before later audience scoring.

### H18 — Organizational dependence does not automatically produce contribution or funding

**Hypothesis:** A transition from use/dependence to employee contribution or financial support requires an internal champion, a recognized dependency/outcome, an acceptable funding target, approval/legal route, and accountability; reliance alone is insufficient.

**Provenance:** Dependency stewardship in [large organizations](../audiences/08-large-organizations-stakeholders.md), maintainer capacity in [11](../audiences/11-django-python-package-maintainers.md), participation in [15](../audiences/15-prospective-existing-django-contributors.md), and organizational funding jobs in [16](../audiences/16-donors-sponsors-corporate-members.md). **Current confidence:** Medium. **Key caveat:** organizational funding reports demonstrate investment but rarely expose failed Django proposals or causal maintenance outcomes. **Test:** Studies 6 and 7; later prioritization should not count all dependent companies as funders or contributors.

## Hypothesis review gate

Before carrying a hypothesis into a final recommendation in any later phase, record:

1. whether it survived a relevant negative comparison;
2. which composed audience contexts it applies to and excludes;
3. whether evidence is behavioral, causal, or only stated/retrospective;
4. which role’s outcome and consequence it describes;
5. whether geography, language, jurisdiction, or access changes it;
6. whether the outcome could rationally be rejection, no-build, migration, bounded participation, declined funding, or retirement.

Absent those checks, these remain research hypotheses rather than positioning or priority conclusions.
